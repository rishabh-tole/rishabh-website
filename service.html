Giving back through education and music with Ekal Vidyalaya

Giving Back with Ekal Vidyalaya

"If the poor cannot come to education, education must reach them at the plow, in the factory, everywhere." - Swami Vivekananda

The first time I read this quote, it changed my perspective on what it means to lack access to education. It showed that this problem extended beyond the fact that some children could not afford education, but that education did not exist in these rural parts of India. Growing up in a family that valued education above all else, I learned firsthand the transformative power it holds. Witnessing the sacrifices my parents made to ensure I had access to quality education opened my eyes to its profound impact on shaping one's life.

Python Summer Camp

I wanted to give back to the community and found that I could use my passion for teaching and knowledge of programming to host a summer camp about the basics of Python. This was a great opportunity to teach the younger generation about programming and how it is an extremely useful skill to have. It was amazing to see a bunch of kids get excited about programming while raising some money for Ekal, allowing me to achieve two goals at once!

My Manch Pravesh for Ekal

I felt empowered knowing the charity I support is making a real difference, giving education to folks in super tough spots that even the government struggles to reach. So, as the Ekal Youth Leader for the Boston chapter, I decided to dedicate my whole Manch Pravesh, a "Music Solo Concert," to Ekal Vidyalaya. We managed to pull in about $2,500 for the cause!

With these funds, we were able to open 24 more schools, educating 720 children in rural India!

What truly moved me was watching the 4th and 5th graders confidently stand on stage, sharing how inspired they were to contribute and help underserved communities. One of the most touching moments came when a 9th grader approached me, saying they were inspired by my efforts and now want to start a dance version of this event. It's amazing how a small act of service can spark such a ripple effect, multiplying the impact beyond what I could have imagined.

Sa Re Ta Re 2023

Music transcends the barrier of language and is the single most potent way to bring people together from around the world. In my family, my grandfather provided opportunities to lesser-known and upcoming artists, and I aim to continue that tradition.

I always longed for a chance to sing on stage but opportunities were scarce, especially when I was very young. While some regional groups organize such competitions, they often cater to specific affinity groups, leaving many without a platform to shine. Since I was just 6 years old, I dreamed of creating a fair stage for young musicians to showcase their talent in any language. Ekal provided me with the opportunity to turn my dream into a reality.

The competition was a huge success, and the fluidity with which it ran was a testament to the countless hours that were poured into planning it. The Judges as well as the audience commented that it felt more like a concert than a competition!

The most remarkable and surprising aspect of this competition was the source of the majority of our funds. The students were incredibly moved by the presentation of the cause, leading them to donate all of their money earned from their various jobs. One student contributed their entire earnings from a summer babysitting job, while another generously donated a significant portion of their income from a dog-sitting business. The satisfaction of giving back is incomparable. Witnessing everyone unite and collaborate towards the goal of educating underprivileged children was truly mesmerizing!
