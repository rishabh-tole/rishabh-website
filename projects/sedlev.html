SedLev

(Sediment Level Detection)

IoT Environmental Tech Machine Learning Energy Harvesting

A smart sensor system to prevent water pollution and street flooding by monitoring storm drain sediment levels.

Problem

Excessive sediment accumulation in storm drain basins can cause water pollution and street flooding. The EPA mandates that sediment levels must not exceed 50%. To avoid these issues and potential fines, towns clean all drains annually, which leads to excessive spending on inspection and cleaning each year.

This approach overlooks the varying sediment buildup rates and lacks a method to detect sudden increases in sediment levels. This problem was identified by my Tech Tiger Team during the 2019-2020 FIRST Lego League competition as part of our Innovation Research project.

Proposed Solution

Initially, we designed a solution with telescopic poles and laser emitters, but it faced issues with murky water and wear.

To address this, I am developing an improved solution using laser distance sensors for our proof of concept. These sensors can measure distances through water and can be mounted at the top of the grate, minimizing wear and maintenance.

For a long-term solution, I am exploring radar distance sensing and self-powered sensors to eliminate external power needs.

Progress

Powering Methods: Piezoelectric Energy Harvesting

Harnessing Energy from Moving Water: By integrating piezoelectric sensors, we can harvest ambient energy from moving water in storm drains. As water flows, it creates stress on the piezoelectric elements, inducing a charge that generates electrical energy to power the sensors autonomously.

Self-Powered Sensors: Utilizing energy harvesters (Magnetic, Solar, Thermal) allows us to create autonomous sensor nodes, reducing maintenance costs.

Predictive Maintenance

By combining sediment-level sensors with environmental data (rainfall, flow rates), we can create a predictive maintenance system.

AI & Stochastic Models: I plan to integrate stochastic processes to model unpredictable water flow and predict sediment accumulation. AI algorithms will identify which drains need maintenance, preventing blockages before they happen.

Markov Decision Processes (MDPs): Using MDPs, SedLev can evaluate multiple factors to make real-time maintenance decisions.

V1 Prototype & Transmission

Detection: I selected laser distance sensors for the first prototype as they can be mounted safely at the top of the grate. Other options like Sonar, Radar, and Physical Spikes were evaluated but deemed less suitable due to cost or wear issues.

Transmission: After discussions with the head of sewage in Walpole, I concluded that Cellular Networks would be the most effective method, consistent with existing utility infrastructure. This ensures reliable, real-time data monitoring via a centralized cloud platform.

Problem Validation

I submitted public records requests to towns like Walpole and Quincy to analyze maintenance costs. My analysis revealed that towns spend substantial amounts on flat-rate annual inspections for all drains, regardless of need.

Implementing SedLev would allow towns to target maintenance only where necessary, generating significant cost savings that would quickly offset the investment in sensor technology.
