Quantum Computing Simulator

C++ Python Linear Algebra Shor's Algorithm

A high-performance simulator built to deepen understanding of quantum mechanics, selected for MIT's Intro to Quantum Computing.

MIT Introduction to Quantum Computing

I was selected as 1 of 164 students from my high school to take the MIT Introduction to Quantum Computing course completely for free. This intensive coursework provided the theoretical foundation needed to understand complex quantum states and operations.

The Project

To better understand the concepts I was learning, I decided to build a quantum computing simulator from scratch. Rather than just studying the theory, I wanted to see the math in action.

Implementation

The simulator is built to model quantum circuits and state vectors. To truly test its capabilities and my understanding, I implemented Shor's Algorithm within the simulator. This required simulating the quantum part of the period-finding subroutine, a key component in factoring large numbers which threatens modern RSA encryption.
